What was challenging about the assignment, and what specific kinds of problems did you have. How did you solve those problems?

I could not understand response protocol for the life of me. I felt silly once I understood, but I had to review my resources many times before understanding.

What did you learn from the assignment? Were there any special insights you had? What did you find that you already knew?

I have tried to work with servers before, but I did not understand enough to actually learn anything that time around. This time also started out with no understanding. I honestly had no specific moments of insight, rather would continue debugging until it would finally work. After it worked, I would break the code on purpose to figure out why it worked. I suppose that could count towards a specific insight, but it was mostly me breaking down the problem until I understood it.

What kinds of resources were helpful for completing the assignment? Specific websites? Lectures? The class Piazza forum? The TAs? How did you use each of these resources?

I rewatched the lecture twice. I also would rewind and skip forward. Without the lecture and the lecture notes, I would not even have a starting point.

What are one or two things you had to Google to complete the assignment?

I Googled a lot this time around, just like I do for most all code projects, but it was not any help this time. I most likely wasn't searching for the correct keywords, but I could not find the answers I was needing. For example, I needed help with writing images to the response. Google simply kept referring me to the http documentation for every question I had. Finally, I figured out to stop converting all of the files to utf8, and the image was served just fine. The only help I got from Google, was when I was confused why my server was taking so long to respond, it referred me to an old stack overflow thread. The solution was that I forgot to write out respond.end()!
